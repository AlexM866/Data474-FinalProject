Some interesting insights from this table:

(Table 1) The majority (81%) of users appear to be from country 29, which according to our documentation is Poland, followed by the Czech Republic (10.9%). It is appropriate to say that the majority of our data comes from the Central European region.

(Table 2 & 3) People in this data set appear to focus their clicks equally among the main product categories (trousers, sale, blouses, skirts) The same can be said for the top 30 clothing models. We note that there is no steep dropoff point among clothing models, though some models are clearly more popular than others.

(Table 4) Black and blue items are the most popular among viewers of this website

(Table 5) People appear to focus on the photos located on the top of the page and there 3.4% drop in clicks between the top middle and bottom middle (3rd most popular) picture locations.

(Table 6) En face model photography photos tended to get more clicks

(Table 7) There is not much difference in clicks between products whose price was greater than the average price of the entire category, and products whose prices were not greater.

(Table 8) The majority of users stayed on either page 1 or 2, and did not venture out to pages 3, 4 or 5.

(Table 9) The majority of clicks (68.3%) are classified as “low-engagement” clicks, or below the mean value for the entire data set.

As we can see from our visuals, a lot of our variables seem to be exponentially distributed or uniformly distributed. The only variable that appears to have an approximately normal distribution is price. We may need to perform some data transformations later on. I don’t have much concern over the uniformly distributed variable, since for a lot of them, it makes sense that they are uniformly distributed (like sid). It is important to take note now, which ones appear to be exponentially distributed, so that we know how to transform them if we encounter performance issues later on: (hi_clicks, model_photography, page, clothing_model, color, country, and order).
